# Tutorial 1: Introduction to Ethics Fundamentals

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this tutorial, you will:
- Understand what ethics is and why it matters
- Learn the three main ethical frameworks
- Identify ethical issues in everyday situations
- Apply ethical thinking to real problems

---

## 📖 What is Ethics?

**Ethics** is the study of right and wrong, good and bad.

### Why Does Ethics Matter?

In college and beyond, you'll face decisions about:
- Academic integrity (cheating vs. honest work)
- Relationships (honesty, loyalty, respect)
- Career choices
- Social issues (justice, fairness, helping others)

---

## 🧭 Three Main Ethical Frameworks

### 1. **Deontological Ethics** (Duty-Based)
**Main Idea:** Some actions are right or wrong in themselves, regardless of the outcome.

**Example:** Lying is wrong, even if it might help someone.

### 2. **Consequentialist Ethics** (Outcome-Based)
**Main Idea:** An action is right if it produces the best outcome for the most people.

**Example:** If lying would save a life, it might be the right choice.

### 3. **Virtue Ethics** (Character-Based)
**Main Idea:** Focus on developing good character and virtues like honesty, courage, and kindness.

**Example:** Ask "What would a virtuous person do?"

---

## ✅ Key Takeaways

1. **Ethics is practical** - It helps us make better decisions daily
2. **Multiple perspectives** - Different frameworks can guide us
3. **Context matters** - The right action depends on the situation
4. **It's learnable** - Ethical thinking is a skill you can develop
5. **Everyone does it** - You're already making ethical choices every day
